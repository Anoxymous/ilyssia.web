Welcome to the space where our love story lives—from dusty 4x4 tracks on Moreton Island to moonlit nursery cuddles and everything in between.

We met unexpectedly, fell in love deliberately, and built a life both wild and wonderful. On 26 September 2026, we’ll marry between sunset and moonrise — a symbol of transition, of rhythm, of our ongoing adventure.

This isn’t the beginning or the end — it’s the moment in between where we choose each other, fully, and invite you to witness it.

Our Story

How We Met

Once upon a beach…

We moved in the same circles for years. Same events. Same adventures. Shared friends. And yet somehow, we never met. Not once.

You’d think fate would’ve thrown us together at a hiking adventure, a climbing event, or a barbecue. But no. It waited. Quietly. Patiently. Watching us orbit each other like the two celestials under the same sky.

Until one day, on a 4x4 trip to Moreton Island — salt in the air, sand under our wheels — we finally collided. We didn’t know it then, but that moment would mark the last day we were strangers. It wasn’t love at first sight. It was something quieter. Slower. Like a tide rolling in. Not in a dramatic, slow-motion way. Just a quiet, curious moment that changed everything.

There was laughter. There was a connection. There was something neither of us could quite explain—something warm and calm and familiar, like finding a piece of home you didn’t know you were missing. That trip became the prologue to everything.

The Proposal

High above the world, love found its voice.

Fast forward to Japan. Snow falling gently. The summit of Maiko Ski Resort stretched out in panoramic silence. It was breathtaking. Timy had a plan, to propose at the top, surrounded by clouds and mountains. Romantic. Serene. Perfect.

Except… there was me—bundled head to toe in full ski gear, determined to look cool in the summit photos. Helmet on. Goggles on. Gloves secured. Hood cinched tight. I was fully committed to the ski aesthetic.

Timy, meanwhile, was gently—and repeatedly—trying to undress me. First, he tried to remove my gloves. I said, “No, my hands will freeze.” Then, he reached for my goggles. “Absolutely not, they look amazing.” Then the helmet. “Why do you want to ruin the photo?”

All he managed to successfully remove were my skis. (Which, in hindsight, was probably a safety precaution in case I panicked and skied away.) Still, despite the layers and the stubbornness, he got down on one knee—me looking like a cool snow trooper—and asked the question that changed everything.

I said yes. Helmet and all. And honestly? It was perfect. Us to a T — a little clumsy, very sincere, and completely unforgettable.

Why Sunset–Moonrise?

Because the in-between is where the magic lives.

Just like us orbiting and finally coming together, we have chosen one of the rare days where the sun and moon conjoin. We chose to be married at sunset—not because it's the end of something, but because it's the most beautiful transition the world offers. The golden hour when everything softens. The moment when light leans into shadow.

There’s also something quietly cosmic about it—a little nod to Timy’s quiet obsession with space. He’s always wanted the moon to be part of the wedding, and honestly… so did I.

And as the sun bows out, the moon quietly rises, mysterious, silver, and certain.

Our sunset marks the closing of two solo paths. Moonrise begins the journey of one shared life. This ceremony isn’t about a fairytale ending or a perfect beginning. It’s about honouring what brought us here and stepping into whatever comes next — moonlit, messy, and meaningful. It’s a rhythm, like our love—gentle, grounded, and full of wonder.

Baby Blip

Our littlest light in the moonlight.

Between the proposal and the wedding, life gave us our greatest adventure yet—our baby girl, Ilyssia. Fondly known as “Blip” during her journey to us, a small nickname for the tiny soul that made a big entrance.

She’s already stolen our sleep, our hearts, and the dance floor.

Having her in our arms as we say our vows feels like the final line of a chapter we never knew we were writing. She is the proof of our love’s becoming—part of our story, our ceremony, and our forever.

Wedding Day Schedule

From Sunset to Moonrise. A Celebration in Reverse.

- 3:30 PM
Guest Arrival, Grazing, Games & Mingling

Step into the evening early, kick off your shoes (if you're brave), and ease into the magic. The sun is still high, the games are out, the food is fresh, and the champagne’s already popping.

Enjoy lawn games, grazing platters, and good company. Think picnic-style mingling with a side of friendly competition.

- 4:00 PM
Bridal Entrance & Blessing Ceremony

Because of the lateness of our ceremony, we didn’t want to miss out on joining our guests in the celebrations and mingling, but we still want to do something special for our arrival. As such, an idea of formed. A blessing ceremony.

Prepare as the beautiful Bride and her lovely bridesmaids arrive in bewildering elegance. A path will be made though the guests for their procession. The guests are invited to participate and give their blessings and best wishes, as they invite the spouse-to-be into the family. A hug or kiss on the cheek or a few words is all the token required.

- 4:15 PM
Group Photos & Sunset Glow-Up

Strike a pose while the golden light is flattering! A casual moment for group shots, selfies, and a little sparkle before we all settle into something more sacred.

Continue to mingle and play while we get all the photo's of family and friends with the bride and groom sorted during this time. Keep an ear out for your turn to come up.

- 5:00 PM
Guests Take Their Seats

Follow the flicker of fairy lights and soft music to the ceremony space. Let the hush fall. It’s time.

- 5:15 PM
Ceremony Begins (Sunset Vows)

As the sun dips, we say our vows—grounded in the day that brought us here, and lifted by the moon that rises with our promises. Unplugged. Intimate. Real.

- 5:45 PM
Drinks & Moonrise Toasts

Celebrate with a twilight toast as the moon begins to peek above. Glasses raised, hearts full. We begin the night with stars in our eyes and bubbles in hand. Offer your congratulations and make your way to the adjacent reception space.

- 6:00 PM
Feast by the Moonlight

Dinner is served! Sit, share, eat, and laugh. Think candlelight, soft music, and something sweet on your plate.

- 8:00 PM
First Dance & Dance Floor Opens

Watch us twirl under the stars — and then join us. Whether you’re a barefoot waltzer or a full-body shuffler, the floor is yours.

Stay, dance, wander, chat, or stargaze. There’s no strict ending, just good people, good music, and a night that unfolds however it wants to.

Questions & Answers

Sunset sealed our past, moonrise begins our forever.

Q: What’s happening before the ceremony?

We’ve planned a relaxed, fun lead-up to the ceremony. Arrive early and enjoy:
- Delicious grazing tables
- Garden games and activities
- Time to catch up, sip something cool, and ease into the evening
- This isn’t just a wedding — it’s a celebration from the moment you arrive.


Q: What should I wear?

Dress as if you’re attending a formal evening garden party — elegant, comfortable, and moonrise-ready. The vibe is: romantic, playful, with a touch of mystery. Wear sunset or moonlight-inspired tones if you feel called — golds, deep blues, silvers, rose hues, or anything that makes you feel celestial.


Q: Will there be food and drinks?

Absolutely! We’ll have delicious bites and flowing drinks from dusk into the night. Think shared feasting, twilight toasts, and sweet treats under the stars.

From arrival to late night, you’ll be well fed and watered.

Pre-ceremony, grazing food platters will be provided and drinks will be availible for purchase.

Post-vows feasting will be a sit-down alternate drop menu and a bar tab will start from 5:45PM.

Let us know any dietary requirements in your RSVP — we’ve got you.


Q: Will there be dancing?

We wouldn’t be us without dancing. Once the vows are spoken and the stars come out, the dance floor opens. We’ll dance from moonrise to midnight — or until our feet (and our baby girl) tell us it’s time to call it a night.


Q: Can I bring my kids, or a Plus One?

While we would love the have everyone possible attend, we unfortunately have strict numbers. Guests are named in the RSVP, with some allowance for those making the travel from overseas. If you’d love to attend but need help with childcare, let us know — we’d love to help make it work for you.


Q: Can I take photos during the ceremony?

We’re having an unplugged ceremony — meaning phones and cameras away, hearts fully present. We’ve hired a photographer to capture every moment, so you can just sit back, soak in the magic, and relive it all later. Snap away during the reception and pre-ceremony celebrations though!


Q: Is there a registry?

There's no registry. We already have all we need and are so grateful simply to have you celebrate with us, that alone means the world. For those who’ve asked, we will have a wishing well at the wedding for cards, handwritten notes, and any contributions toward our future adventures as a family. There’s no expectation, just love.

Whether you gift us with words, wisdom, or a quiet smile under the moonlight… we thank you deeply.


Q: When do I have to RSVP by?

Please let us know by 4th Jan 2026.

You can RSVP via withjoy.com or alternately send us a direct message so we can mark you as attending.
